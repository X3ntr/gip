IT-Autobiography

Hi, my name is Sander Forrer also known as X3ntr. I'm a 18 year old from Belgium, currently studying informatics at Immaculata Instituut Oostmalle.

I came in touch with computers and games at the age of 7. My mom used educational programs/games to teach me and my 3 brothers math and spelling,... Two years later, I saw my stephbrother play Age Of Empires. From that moment I started playing simple games on CD-ROM. What roused my interests were the possibilities.
All those things you can't do in real life were made possible and although my mom had set a limit on the time I could play, I really started to like it. It was like living a second life.
Turning 14 was a major milestone in my early gamer-life. Me and my twin brother got our first laptop to share for our birthday. I started to play multiplayer and was allowed to create a Facebook account.
The years after I got more games, used the computer daily for school work and entertainment and got my own laptop.
It was then I encountered the first problems with game compatibility and small software issues.
This wakened my interest in how computers work and how software and games are put together. My teachers back then had often very little computer knowledge and whenever there occured a problem me or one of my brothers used to fix it.
I gamed a lot with my best buddy and we explored the online communities, participated in forum discussions and suddenly I heard about "hacks" and "hackers".
This immediately got my attention and I started reading articles, books about programming in c++ and even biographies for example Kevin Mitnick.
I realized there are a lot of difficulties and obstructions on the road of becoming a hacker and one of the biggest is where do I start? That's when I decided to change school and attend Immaculata Instituut to prepare for University.

Today I usually spend about 8 to 10 or even 12 hours a day behind my pc. The major amount of time I play games or watch gamestreams, YouTube and communicate with my friends online.
The time left I spend figuring things out, making homework or keeping up with the latest games, hardware. I really feel like there has been an evolution.
I started out with playing simple games, now I write very simple games myself. I assembled three pc's so far and set up my own virtual lab.
I crashed my laptop over and over again messing with settings, digging in the registry and writing simple batch scripts.
I successfully hacked wifi-hotspots with BackTrack and Kali Linux, I've build up a life online in the game and stream community.
I recently setup my own stream. Going through this I've encountered a lot of problems and eventually solved almost all of them.
I've shared solutions with others and so I started helping others out and give advice to those who ask.
For example a lot of streamers are looking for custom build gaming pc's but they're often confused about what to buy or where to buy.
I don't consider myself an expert and I do say so if I'm not sure about something or haven't heard of it.

I expect IT will become a part of our life we can't live without. Based on that we'll need people to create, design, support, secure and maintain it.
Alongside the integration of IT we'll need to introduce new laws and have people to ensure those.
The more IT becomes part of our daily life the more risks come with it and as they say you have to fight fire with fire, I want to become an ethical hacker and hope to attend MIT.

IT and computers have become a major part of my life, not only for entertainment but for future job chances and social life as well.
It provides me the possibilities and tools for common problem solving and communicating with the world, assists me when playing guitar or the drums and helps me e-shopping and managing my financials.
I still haven't found the girlfriend option though.
